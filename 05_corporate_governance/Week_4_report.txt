WEEK 4 REPORT – SUPPLIER PERFORMANCE & SEGMENTATION
Objective

To evaluate supplier quality, cost efficiency, and performance using statistical analysis, correlation study, and segmentation techniques.

Activities Performed

- Analyzed vendor defect rates to identify high-risk and low-risk suppliers.
- Studied the relationship between procurement cost and defect rate using correlation analysis.
- omputed supplier efficiency metrics to evaluate cost-effectiveness.
- Segmented suppliers into performance categories based on cost and defect benchmarks.
- Generated visualizations including scatter plots, distributions, heatmaps, and boxplots for performance interpretation.

Key Findings

- Defect rates across suppliers were consistent, with minor variation between vendors.
- Correlation between cost and defect rate was negligible, indicating that higher cost does not guarantee better quality.
- Efficiency analysis highlighted a small group of highly optimized suppliers delivering better value.
- Supplier segmentation classified vendors into five categories: Top Performers, High Risk, Premium Suppliers, Average Performers, and Low Cost Risky.
- A balanced distribution of suppliers across segments indicates diversified supplier behavior.

Outcome

- Identified high-risk vendors requiring quality improvement.
- Recognized top-performing suppliers suitable for long-term partnerships.
- Established a structured supplier evaluation model based on cost, defect rate, and efficiency.
- Prepared dataset and insights for advanced logistics and decision-making analysis in subsequent stages.

Conclusion

Week 4 successfully transformed raw supplier data into actionable insights through statistical evaluation and segmentation. The analysis provides a strong foundation for supplier optimization and strategic decision-making in logistics operations.
